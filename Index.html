TRUTH & RECONCILIATION

The Path to Reconciliation

Addressing the legacy of residential schools and building a better future together

2008

Truth and Reconciliation Commission Established

Created through the Indian Residential Schools Settlement Agreement with $72 million in federal support to document the history and harms of residential schools.

2008-2015

Commission Gathers Testimonies

Over 6 years, the TRC gathered testimonies from 6,500 Survivors and witnesses, hosted 7 national events, and reviewed over 5 million federal records.

2015

94 Calls to Action Released

The Final Report and 94 Calls to Action were released, providing Canada's roadmap for reconciliation across all sectors of society.

2019

Indigenous Languages Act

Royal Assent received, addressing Calls to Action 13-15 and establishing framework for language preservation.

2021

National Day for Truth and Reconciliation

Established as federal statutory holiday on September 30 to honor residential school Survivors and remember the children who never came home.

2025

Progress Continues

More than 85% of the 76 federal Calls to Action are completed or well underway. Reconciliation remains an ongoing journey requiring sustained effort.
